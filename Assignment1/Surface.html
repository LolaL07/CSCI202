Moon Jellyfish - The surface

Just beneath the surface sea, barely a touch from the sky, floats a translucent lavender jellyfish. The opaque white clover on the top of its bell marks its true name as that of a Moon Jellyfish. In the median of two currents, it consumes each small krill drift within its grasp. The others small things that it may eat are pulled into the currents. A crab larvae swims into the eastern one that pulls in an arc towards the docks along the shore. A short distance away, many fish swim into the western current that gently leads them to bluer seas.
